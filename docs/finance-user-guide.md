# Finance Dashboard User Guide

## Overview

The Finance Dashboard provides comprehensive insights into your sales performance, product analytics, and financial forecasting. This guide will help you understand and utilize all the features available in the dashboard.

## Getting Started

### Accessing the Dashboard

1. Log in to your KalaBandhu account
2. Navigate to the Finance section from the main menu
3. Select "Dashboard" to view the main analytics

### Dashboard Layout

The dashboard is organized into several key sections:

- **Summary Cards**: Key metrics at a glance
- **Sales Overview**: Revenue trends and performance
- **Product Performance**: Top-performing products
- **Analytics**: Forecasting and insights
- **Insights**: AI-powered recommendations

## Key Metrics

### Summary Cards

- **Total Revenue**: Total sales revenue for the selected period
- **Total Orders**: Number of orders placed
- **Units Sold**: Total units sold
- **Performance**: Overall performance indicator

## Sales Analytics

### Viewing Sales Trends

1. **Time Range Selection**: Choose from 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or 1 year
2. **Resolution**: View data daily, weekly, or monthly
3. **Chart Types**: Switch between line, area, and bar charts

### Interpreting Sales Data

- **Revenue Trend**: Shows how your sales are performing over time
- **Order Volume**: Number of orders received
- **Average Order Value**: Revenue per order
- **Growth Rate**: Percentage change from previous period

## Product Performance

### Analyzing Product Data

1. **Sort Options**:
   - Revenue: Sort by total sales
   - Units: Sort by quantity sold
   - Growth: Sort by growth rate
   - Margin: Sort by profit margin

2. **View Types**:
   - **Table**: Detailed product list
   - **Chart**: Visual bar chart
   - **Pie**: Category breakdown

### Product Metrics

- **Revenue**: Total sales for the product
- **Units Sold**: Quantity sold
- **Average Price**: Price per unit
- **Growth Rate**: Sales growth percentage
- **Margin**: Profit margin percentage
- **Rank**: Product performance ranking

## Forecasting

### Revenue Forecasting

1. **Select Time Horizon**: Choose 7, 14, 30, or 60 days
2. **Choose Metric**: Revenue, orders, or quantity
3. **Confidence Level**: 80%, 90%, 95%, or 99%

### Understanding Forecasts

- **Historical Data**: Past performance (solid line)
- **Forecast**: Predicted future performance (dashed line)
- **Confidence Interval**: Upper and lower bounds (shaded area)
- **Today Marker**: Current date reference line

### Forecast Accuracy

- **High Accuracy** (>90%): Reliable predictions
- **Medium Accuracy** (70-90%): Moderate reliability
- **Low Accuracy** (<70%): Limited reliability

## Loan Management

### Applying for a Loan

1. **Navigate to Loans**: Click "Loans" in the finance menu
2. **Start Application**: Click "Apply for Loan"
3. **Fill Personal Information**: Provide required details
4. **Business Information**: Enter business details
5. **Loan Details**: Specify amount and purpose
6. **Upload Documents**: Submit required documents
7. **Submit Application**: Review and submit

### Required Documents

- **Identity Proof**: Aadhaar Card or PAN Card
- **Address Proof**: Utility bill or bank statement
- **Business Proof**: GST certificate or business license
- **Financial Statements**: Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet
- **Bank Statements**: Last 6 months statements

### Loan Status Tracking

- **Draft**: Application not submitted
- **Submitted**: Under review
- **Under Review**: Being processed
- **Approved**: Loan approved
- **Rejected**: Application rejected
- **Disbursed**: Funds transferred

## Glossary

### Common Terms

- **AOV (Average Order Value)**: Total revenue divided by number of orders
- **Churn Rate**: Percentage of customers who stop buying
- **Conversion Rate**: Percentage of visitors who make purchases
- **Gross Margin**: Revenue minus cost of goods sold
- **ROI (Return on Investment)**: Profit divided by investment cost

### Financial Terms

- **Working Capital**: Funds available for daily operations
- **Cash Flow**: Movement of money in and out of business
- **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: Total debt divided by total equity
- **EBITDA**: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization
